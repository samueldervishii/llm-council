Understanding Cortex's
Personalization Features

Cortex has a handful of profile settings you can use to make responses feel a bit closer to the way you actually work. Nothing fancy, just enough context so you are not re-explaining yourself in every chat.

Profile preferences

These are account-wide and live in your profile settings:

  • Display name & username — how Cortex addresses you, and how you show up in any sessions you share.
  • Field of work — a short hint about what you do, so the tone and depth of answers can land in the right neighbourhood. An engineer, a grad student, and a marketer all want slightly different things out of the same question.
  • Personal preferences — a free-form line or two that travels with every chat. Things like “I write in Python and TypeScript”, “keep answers short”, or “explain machine-learning concepts like I am new”.

How it actually works

When you send a message, Cortex quietly includes your profile context in the background. That means Claude can:

  • dial the technical depth up or down to match your field,
  • follow the communication style you prefer (short vs. thorough, formal vs. casual),
  • remember the tools you use without you repeating them,
  • and reach for examples that make sense in your world.

Your preferences apply to every conversation automatically — no need to reintroduce yourself each time.

Getting better answers

A few things that tend to help:

  • Be specific. “Third-year CS student writing a thesis on NLP” is much more useful than “student”.
  • List your tools. Languages, frameworks, editors — the more Claude knows, the more the code examples will look like yours.
  • Say how you like to be talked to. If you want bullet points, say so. If you want step-by-step, say that.
  • Update it when things change. Your interests, tools, and focus shift over time. The preferences should too.

Privacy

Profile info stays tied to your account and is only used to shape your own conversations. It is not shared with other users or sent anywhere beyond what the chat itself needs.

You can change or wipe it any time from your profile settings.
